A brief timeline of my life
(so far)

  • I was born in Washington, DC.

    August 8, 1993

    My parents were graduate students in the DC area when they had me. Thus, by the 14th amendment, I am a citizen of the United States.

  • I moved to Ottawa, Canada.

    Some time in 1995

    After my parents completed their education, my family moved to Canada to join my grandparents who had already been living there.

  • I moved to Jakarta, Indonesia.

    Some time in 1997

    My grandparents moved back to Indonesia, and soon my family followed suit. After all, this is our motherland.

  • I moved to Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.

    August 2001

    Due to many reasons including a recent overthrow of a 32 year regime, my parents decided it was best that we moved to Malaysia for a better quality of life.

  • I was accepted to UT Austin.

    February 2011

    I received a letter stating I had been accepted to go to UT as a Mechanical Engineering major. Prior to applying I had never heard of the school, and I picked the major from a list because it sounded like the coolest engineering major. Needless to say, I was not the best decision maker at the time.

  • I graduated High School.

    May 2011

    I finished my A Levels, thereby graduating from high school. It didn't quite feel like an achievement, but apparently it was supposed to be so I included it here.

  • I moved to Austin, Texas.

    August 2011

    When I first arrived, I only knew one person in the whole state, and that was my roommate Armand.

  • I began college as a Mechanical Engineering major.

    Fall 2011

    I had no idea what I was doing. I made friends with others who were on the same boat. Together, our ship sailed.

  • I switched my major to Computer Science.

    Summer 2013

    Two years later I still didn't know what I was doing with Mechanical Engineering. All I knew was the only classes I enjoyed were the C++ and MATLAB programming classes, so I decided to switch my major. Best decision I ever made.

  • I was part of the winning team of Bloomberg Code B.

    January 2015

    I collaborated with two other students in an algorithmic trading hackathon hosted by Bloomberg. Our team led in the beginning, and then we were losing for the next 12 hours until we took a surprise victory and doubled what was then the high score. It is through this competition that I won the Chromebook that I used to build this website.

  • I tried to enter the world of algorithmic trading.

    Spring - Summer 2015

    After Bloomberg opened my eyes to the world of algorithmic trading, I spent several months working with a successful day trader to implement his strategy into an algorithmic trading program in MQL4 to use with the FXCM MetaTrader 4 forex trading platform. Despite promising results, the project was suspended due to a lack of funds for investment.

  • I became a freshman in real life.

    December 2015

    After 4.5 years in college - of which 2.5 years was in CS - I now have a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science. Yay.
